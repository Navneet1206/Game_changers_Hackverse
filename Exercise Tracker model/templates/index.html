Pushups

Pushups are a fundamental bodyweight exercise that targets the chest, shoulders, triceps, and core.

Proper Posture
  • Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  • Place your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor.
  • Push yourself back up to the starting position.
Rules
  • Avoid arching your back or sagging your hips.
  • Breathe in as you lower your body and breathe out as you push up.
Benefits
  • Strengthens upper body and core muscles.
  • Improves cardiovascular health.
  • Enhances overall endurance.
Squats

Squats are a compound exercise that targets the legs, glutes, and lower back.

Proper Posture
  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Lower your body by bending your knees and hips.
  • Keep your chest up and back straight.
  • Go as low as you can while maintaining proper form.
Rules
  • Do not let your knees go past your toes.
  • Keep your weight on your heels.
Benefits
  • Builds lower body strength.
  • Improves mobility and balance.
  • Boosts calorie burn.
Jumping Jacks

Jumping jacks are a full-body exercise that improves cardiovascular fitness.

Proper Posture
  • Start with your feet together and arms at your sides.
  • Jump while spreading your legs and raising your arms above your head.
  • Return to the starting position in one fluid motion.
Rules
  • Land softly to avoid joint stress.
  • Maintain a steady rhythm.
Benefits
  • Improves heart health.
  • Enhances coordination.
  • Burns calories effectively.
Planks

Planks are an isometric core exercise that strengthens the abs, back, and shoulders.

Proper Posture
  • Start in a forearm plank position.
  • Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  • Engage your core and hold the position.
Rules
  • Do not let your hips sag or rise too high.
  • Breathe steadily throughout the exercise.
Benefits
  • Strengthens the core muscles.
  • Improves posture and stability.
  • Reduces risk of back pain.
